# Design Context

## Users
Retail traders (primary) and trading mentors (secondary). Mentors are independent
educators who run paid mentor spaces; traders are learners evaluating mentors,
booking 1-on-1 sessions, buying webinar tickets, and consuming announcements.
Mixed sophistication: some are seasoned prop-firm traders with strong opinions
on UI density, others are newer learners who need guidance. Used in long
focused sessions on desktop (mentors managing their dashboard) and shorter
mobile sessions (students consuming content, browsing the directory).

## Brand Personality
Three words: **trustworthy, focused, alive**.
- Voice: confident without being aggressive. No hype. Direct.
- Tone: editorial-pragmatic — financial-services credibility but lighter than
  Bloomberg-style density. Closer to Linear / Vercel-app-style restraint than
  to a Wall Street terminal.
- Emotional goals: students should feel they're in capable hands; mentors
  should feel their work is being taken seriously and that growth is
  reachable. Both should feel rewarded for engagement.

## Aesthetic Direction
- Theme: light by default, dark mode supported. Dark must not lean on neon
  glow or AI-cyan accents.
- References: Linear (motion + density restraint), Stripe Dashboard (data
  legibility, trust), Notion's marketing pages (warm-restrained typography).
- Anti-references: generic AI dashboards (Vercel template clones), trading
  platform Bloomberg-Terminal density, fintech dark+neon clichés, Web3
  rainbow gradients.
- Existing tokens: `glass-card`, OKLCH-friendly CSS variables, primary accent
  per-mentor (configurable, used as inset border on hero cards).

## Design Principles
1. **Clarity beats density.** Trading platforms are tempted to cram. Resist —
   mentors need primary actions obvious, students need conversion paths
   uncluttered.
2. **Trust signals are first-class.** Verified badges, cancellation policies,
   risk disclaimers, and DSA controls must be present without screaming. They
   are calm furniture, not alarms.
3. **Reward progress.** Setup checklists, capacity pills, "filling fast"
   spotlight badges — every interaction surface should give a reason to come
   back. Avoid bare "you have 0 of X" dead-ends.
4. **One accent per surface.** Mentor's brand accent is the only colored
   element. Status (success/warning/danger) uses semantic color but sparingly.
5. **Motion confirms, never decorates.** Toasts fade, sticky panels slide,
   spotlight cross-fades — but no hover-reveal flourishes that don't tell the
   user something useful.
